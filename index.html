Pedro Pacheco
Software Engineer @ Spotify
Pedro Pacheco
Software Engineer @ Spotify

Scaling music metadata distribution using Hadoop

New music metadata is added daily to Spotify's catalog, both from external providers and from internal sources of data. All the metadata for tracks, albums and artists needs to be processed and efficiently served to our users. As the amount of content increases, we need to adapt our backend to accommodate for the growth.
This talk will be focused on Spotify's backend for handling music metadata and on how we scaled one of the metadata datasets using Hadoop.

Pedro is a backend engineer that has been working with Spotify's music metadata systems for the past 3 years. He has also worked at OutSystems and Portugal Telecom, after graduating from FEUP in 2010. Originally from the Porto area, you can now find him living in Stockholm. When not building software, Pedro will most likely be in the nearest swimming pool, enjoying some craft beer or travelling around the world.

Pros and cons of Scala as a server-side programming language at ShiftForward

ShiftForward has been using Scala almost since its inception, in 2011. During the past few years, both the language and the way ShiftForward uses it has evolved. This talk aims to explain ShiftForward's experiences with Scala: why we decided to go with it in the first place, what benefits we take from it, how it affects our programming style and what issues we have encountered in using it.

João graduated from FEUP in 2010 with a Master in Informatics and Computing Engineering. Since 2012, he's a Software Engineer at ShiftForward, a company in the online advertising industry, where he's focused on building distributed, responsive and scalable systems, as well as applying machine learning techniques to solve some of the online advertising industry's problems.

Android development: The good, the Bad and the Ugly.

Looking back at the first web application development: problems and solutions

3 years after the launch of a fast-growing web application, we look back at some of the worse difficulties encountered. From database to cache, from Ruby to Java, from trivial to not so simple solutions, we analyse the problems (and solutions) a 'rookie' team encountered when building their first real web application.

Pedro is a web programmer at heart. After 7 years building windows apps in VB6 and C#, and building websites in PHP for fun at home, he made the jump to Ruby on Rails. 5 years gone, and he hasn't looked back since.
